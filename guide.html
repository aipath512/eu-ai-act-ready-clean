How it works — in plain words

No jargon. Here’s exactly what we do.

If you run a business and you’re not a tech expert, this page is for you. Five minutes and you’ll understand the whole thing.

First, the simple version.

A new EU law wants companies to be honest and clear about how they use AI — and to put proof of it where people can see it: on your website. Right now most websites just say things. Soon they’ll need to show things. We add that proof for you, without rebuilding your site.

What we do — in 4 steps
1

We look at your website

We check the part everyone can see and find what’s missing — no clear AI statement, no one named as responsible, no proof anyone can verify. We call this your gap.

2

We write your proof in plain words

We prepare a clear, honest set of statements: where you use AI, who’s in charge of it, your rules, and where your information comes from. You read it, you approve it. Nothing goes live without your okay.

3

We add it to your website

We place the proof on your site and lock it so it can’t be quietly changed — anyone checking can confirm it’s genuine. We don’t touch the rest of your website. It keeps looking exactly as it does today.

4

It keeps working on its own

From then on, your proof is always there for customers, buyers, regulators, and AI tools to check — automatically, around the clock. When something needs updating, we handle it.

What we need from you · What you get

What we need from you

  • Your website address
  • A few minutes to confirm how you use AI
  • One approval before anything goes live

What you get

  • A website that’s ready for the new EU AI law
  • Proof customers and buyers can trust
  • The AI-friendly layer included, free
  • Less risk of losing deals over an evidence gap

One thing we’ll always be straight about

We don’t certify that you obey the law. No honest company can do that for you. What we do is make your proof visible, organised, and easy to check — so you’re ready, and so the people checking can see it for themselves.

› Want the technical detail? (open this)
Under the hood, the proof layer is built from machine-readable evidence files — ai-proof.json and authority.json. Each file is sealed with a SHA-256 fingerprint (a unique signature that changes if even one character is altered, so tampering is obvious) and an OpenTimestamps anchor (an independent, tamper-proof record of when it was published). This is what lets AI systems and auditors read and verify your evidence directly — instead of just trusting a sentence on a page. It’s deployed at the edge, so your existing website is never rebuilt or slowed down. Happy to walk your developer through it on a call.
