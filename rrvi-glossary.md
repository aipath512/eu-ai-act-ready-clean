# Purpose

This glossary defines the official terminology used within the EU AI Act Compliance Management System™ and RRVI™ repository.

The objective is to maintain consistent understanding between humans, organizations, AI systems and compliance documentation.



---

# AI System

## Definition

A machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and that may generate outputs such as predictions, recommendations, decisions or content.

## Related concepts

- AI Use Case
- AI System Owner
- Risk Classification
- Human Oversight



---

# AI Governance

## Definition

The framework of policies, procedures, responsibilities and controls used to manage AI systems throughout their lifecycle.

## Includes

- Accountability
- Risk management
- Documentation
- Monitoring
- Oversight



---

# AI Governance Owner

## Definition

The individual responsible for coordinating the organization's AI governance activities and compliance structure.



---

# AI System Owner

## Definition

The individual responsible for a specific AI system throughout its lifecycle.

Responsibilities include:

- ownership
- monitoring
- documentation
- risk management



---

# Audit

## Definition

A systematic, independent and documented process for evaluating compliance with defined requirements.



---

# Checklist

## Definition

A controlled document used to verify completion of required activities.



---

# Compliance

## Definition

The state of conforming with applicable legal, regulatory and organizational requirements.



---

# Controlled Document

## Definition

A document that is uniquely identified, versioned, approved and managed under document control.



---

# Declaration

## Definition

A formal statement confirming implementation or completion of a compliance activity.



---

# Evidence

## Definition

Objective information demonstrating that an activity has been performed.

Examples:

- records
- approvals
- logs
- reports
- screenshots
- documents



---

# High-Risk AI System

## Definition

An AI system classified as High-Risk according to Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.



---

# Human Oversight

## Definition

Measures ensuring that natural persons appropriately supervise AI system operation where required.



---

# Incident

## Definition

An event affecting the availability, integrity, confidentiality, compliance or safe operation of an AI system.



---

# Inspection

## Definition

A formal review performed by a regulatory authority or authorized body to verify compliance.



---

# Policy

## Definition

A high-level statement establishing management intentions, direction and principles.



---

# Procedure

## Definition

A documented sequence of activities describing how a process is performed.



---

# Register

## Definition

A controlled record containing operational compliance information.



---

# Risk Classification

## Definition

The process of determining the applicable EU AI Act risk category for an AI system or AI use case.



## Risk Categories

- Prohibited
- High-Risk
- Limited Risk
- Minimal Risk



---

# Traceability

## Definition

The ability to follow relationships between requirements, controls, documents, records, evidence and declarations.

## RRVI principle

Every compliance statement should be traceable to supporting evidence.



---

# Version Control

## Definition

The systematic management of document revisions throughout their lifecycle.

Includes:

- version identification
- approval
- history
- status management



---

# RRVI™

## Full name

Remote Regulatory Verification Infrastructure™


## Definition

A structured compliance infrastructure designed to organize regulatory knowledge, operational controls, documents and evidence for verification.



---

# EU AI Act Compliance Management System™

## Definition

A structured approach for managing AI Act obligations through governance, documentation, evidence and traceability.



---

# Evidence Package

## Definition

A collection of controlled documents and supporting evidence prepared for review or verification.



---

# Inspector Package

## Definition

A structured package containing relevant compliance information, documentation and evidence available for regulatory verification.



---

# SHA-256 Integrity

## Definition

A cryptographic fingerprint mechanism used to verify that a digital artifact has not been modified.



---

# Timestamp

## Definition

A trusted record of when a document, artifact or event existed.



---

# No Orphan Principle

## Definition

Every compliance entity must have valid relationships to its context.

Examples:

- Document → Module
- Evidence → Document
- AI Use Case → Risk Category
